It is labeled as "α-gal allergy", defined as: "A type of IgE-mediated allergic reaction to galactose-α-1,3-galactose (α-gal) characterized by delayed onset of symptoms, typically triggered by mammalian meat consumption and associated with sensitization from Lone Star tick bites.", also known as "galactose-α-1,3-galactose allergy", "mammalian meat allergy", related terms include "delayed anaphylactic reaction", and narrower terms include "cetuximab hypersensitivity".
